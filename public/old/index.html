Album

In The Lab

Belgian electronic music visionary Benoît Dobbelstein returns with his most ambitious release to date: In the Lab, a genre-blending expedition through sound that blurs the lines between science and soul, circuitry and emotion. The album is available now on all major streaming platforms.

Drawing influence from electronic pioneers like William Orbit, Orbital, and Boards Of Canada, Dobbelstein sculpts a vivid audio world that is at once warm and uplifting, yet dark and mysterious. In the Lab is a study in contrasts — dreamy and dramatic, quirky and cinematic, moody yet bright — capturing the full emotional range of the human experience through an experimental, high-tech lens.

Each track pulses with percussive energy, unfolding like scenes from an invisible film. Synth textures glide across layered rhythms, creating atmospheres that feel intense and reflective, cool and sexy, tense yet playful. There is an unmistakable narrative arc here, one that evokes themes of investigation, innovation, and inner discovery.

"In the Lab is about experimentation, not just with sound but with emotion. It's a sonic microscope into the modern psyche — full of friction, beauty, absurdity, and breakthrough."— Benoît J. Dobbelstein

Whether you're diving into headphones or lighting up a dancefloor, In the Lab is not just an album — it's an auditory experience, perfectly engineered for late-night journeys, cinematic moods, or moments of solitary reflection.

Track Highlights

"Bombers Vest" — A dark, rhythmic opener that builds from minimal pulses to full-bodied orchestrations.

"DNA" — Cool, sexy, and investigative — perfect for noir-tinged narratives.

"Grave Danger" — A quirky, dramatic interlude that fuses real drums with futuristic glitches.

"Sursurrus" — A mysterious and powerful closer that leaves you suspended in sonic space.

About

Benoît J.
Dobbelstein

Music Composer

Fascinated by music since his early childhood, BJD has been investigating its emotional scale and power through composition, most of all to picture. Underlining the narrative, reinforcing it by the selection of the right chords and notes at the right time, is what gives him the greatest kick. Equally, it keeps him going for more, eager to try and to learn every day, with every new project.

For over 20 years, BJD has been working as a composer for film and TV, advertising and corporate communication. He feels at home in many genres, especially in alternative pop/rock, electronic music (80's!) and orchestral hybrids. Being also a producer and engineer, he's a solid "one-stop" value of the Belgian music industry. Former board member of SABAM (Belgian PRO), he's also been involved with music publishing since 2007 through his company dB minor music.

"To achieve great things, two things are needed; a plan, and not quite enough time." — Leonard Bernstein
